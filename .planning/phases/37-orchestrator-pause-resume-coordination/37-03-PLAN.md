<objective>
Build the two orchestrator consumers that drive the Quartz-owned three-state model in response to
Keeper's signals. Pause halts the cron via `PauseJob` (D-08); Resume — only if the trigger is `Paused`
— deletes the stale paused job and schedules a fresh from-now trigger off L1's cron (D-06/D-03,
sidestepping misfire). Concurrency safety is `ConcurrentMessageLimit = 1` + idempotent Quartz
transitions + redelivery-on-crash (D-07) — NO dedicated lock, NO reference-counting set, and
deliberately NOT the `IWorkflowL1Store` `Wait(0)` drop-if-held stripe (it would silently lose a Resume).

Purpose: Deliver PAUSE-02 (halt), PAUSE-03 (reschedule-from-L1), PAUSE-04 (idempotent/serial),
PAUSE-05/D-09 (resume only when Paused; None/Normal ignored). The orchestrator half of the phase.
Output: Two consumers + their definitions + Program.cs wiring + two thin lifecycle seams; flips Plan
01's consumer/scheduling tests GREEN.

## Trust Boundaries

| Boundary | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Bus → Orchestrator consumer | Pause/Resume messages cross from the trusted internal bus into the single-replica orchestrator's Quartz scheduler. Untrusted only in the sense of duplicate/out-of-order delivery, not external attacker. |

## STRIDE Threat Register

| Threat ID | Category | Component | Disposition | Mitigation Plan |
|-----------|----------|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| T-37-04 | Denial of Service | Lost Resume → workflow stranded paused forever (THE core availability risk D-07 addresses) | mitigate | NO drop-if-held `Wait(0)` stripe; consumer returns→ACK only after the Quartz transition, so a crash-before-ack leaves the message un-acked → redelivered → reprocessed. `ConcurrentMessageLimit = 1` serializes. Idempotent transitions make replay a no-op. |
| T-37-05 | Tampering/Repudiation | Log injection via `H`/`WorkflowId` in the two new consumer log lines | mitigate | `{WorkflowId}`/`{H}` are STRUCTURED template holes, never interpolated (existing repo idiom T-35-04/05; enforced in acceptance criteria). |
| T-37-06 | Elevation of Privilege | A racing Stop (DeleteJob) vs Resume | mitigate | Resume acts only on `GetTriggerStateAsync == Paused`; a Stop having already `DeleteJob`'d yields `None` → Resume no-ops. Quartz's own job-store concurrency serializes the racing DeleteJob vs the Resume reschedule (D-06). |
| T-37-07 | Spoofing | Forged Resume resuming a workflow an operator Stopped | mitigate | Resume ignores `None` (Stopped) — a Stopped workflow stays Stopped until an operator `Start` (D-06). A forged Resume on a Stopped workflow is a no-op. |
</threat_model>
