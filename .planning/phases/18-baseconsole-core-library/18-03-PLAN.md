<objective>
Build the embedded health-probe surface and the `AddBaseConsole` composition root. This includes the
inner minimal-Kestrel `IHostedService` that exposes `/health/live|ready|startup`, the resolution of the
inner-Kestrel ↔ outer-bus wiring (RESEARCH Open-Q 1), the outer-host health-check registration, and the
non-generic `AddBaseConsole(cfg)` chain that ties Redis + health together.

Purpose: Delivers CONSOLE-HEALTH-01 (embedded HTTP listener), CONSOLE-HEALTH-02 (`/health/live` = 200,
self-only, never touches deps), CONSOLE-HEALTH-03 (`/health/ready` reflects bus state via the outer
`IBusHealth`), CONSOLE-HEALTH-04 (duplicated gate + StartupHealthCheck served), and CONSOLE-01 (the
`AddBaseConsole`/RunAsync-style chain wired in a handful of lines).

**Open-Q 1 resolution (LOCKED for this plan):** The inner Kestrel has its own DI container, so it cannot
see the outer host's auto-registered MassTransit `ready` check directly. Resolution: the embedded service
receives the outer `IServiceProvider`; it registers a custom `BusReadyHealthCheck` in the inner DI that
resolves the outer `IBusHealth` (or `IBus.CheckHealth()`) at check time and surfaces its result tagged
`"ready"`. The outer `IStartupGate` singleton is shared into the inner DI by instance. This keeps
`/health/live` self-only (no bus/Redis) while `/health/ready` tracks real bus state. Plan 04 proves it.

Output: The embedded listener, the bus-ready bridge check, the outer health registration, and the
`AddBaseConsole` root.
</objective>

| T-18-11 | Tampering | `/health/ready` not reflecting true bus state (silent false-Healthy) | mitigate | `BusReadyHealthCheck` reads the outer `IBusHealth.CheckHealth()` and returns Unhealthy when the bus is not resolvable/started; Plan 04 proves `/health/ready` tracks bus state. Prevents a stale-Healthy readiness that would route traffic to a disconnected console. |
